My Coding Projects

HUEY - Swift

I created the idea for 2021 HUEY because of my skating community in Jackson Heights, Queens. Every day in the summer, we would head outside together to the Maloof Skatepark in Flushing Meadows and spend hours perfecting old tricks, learning new techniques, and generally having fun. This community of teens and the occasional child prodigy became my home away from home as we all accepted each other at our lows and highs. However, one underlying, yet extremely pervasive issue surfaced for me that summer in seeing just how many of my peers at the skatepark didn’t have enough money for food to fuel their bodies. If there was some money, we’d split french fries and red bulls between all of us. But doing a sport like skateboarding for hours on end without enough (or the correct) nutrients can be extremely detrimental to one's health over time. I wanted to find a way to help solve this issue that people around me were facing. Especially because this problem was only reflective of the larger issue of food disparity in New York. I thought it unfair that there’s an abundance of access to inexpensive, healthy food in certain neighborhoods and an utter lack of choice in others. So I began to think of ways to solve this in the ways that I knew how.


In 2022, The Alzar school awarded me a grant to begin working on HUEY, a hydroponics system for my community in Queens that will help local folks get more access to green food. I then enlisted the sponsorship of Google LLC to help continuously fund the purchase or materials and new tech to aid me in this mission. I’ve programmed an app that will aid me in monitoring my plants within my physical hydroponics system from afar using a Raspberry Pi. The app also has an average user interface that allows folks at home to identify the plants growing in their gardens and how to best take care of them. In the coming year, my focus will shift towards the 3D printed construction of my hydroponics towers.


BitLifeHome - Python

I love to code for fun and test my knowledge. I’ve found that, even if I’ve taken a course in a coding language, the only way I’ll retain that information is through constant practice, trial, and error. That’s why I work on fun terminal games like my BitLifeHome (working title) in my free time. If you’re familiar with the original Bit Life, you’ll understand that this terminal game I’m developing gives a player a fake life story and allows them to move through their little digital world autonomously. You get to make decisions about how to spend your money, who to be friends with, where you work etc. Working on this game has definitely taught me a lot about what I do and do not know about Python (my favorite language). I definitely have a long way to go!

Personal Wesbite - HTML, CSS, JS

But I’m going to keep on going because this is what I love and it's therapeutic to see the fruits of my hard labor.

My Academic Projects

For my 2023-2024 school year, I am being endorsed by the Chapin School Individual Study Committee to film, produce, and edit a documentary on the subject of cybersecurity. My thesis question, which has been posed alongside other supplemental questions to my interviewees in the professional cybersecurity community, is “Should we trust our computers, and why?”. I aim to uncover the ins and outs of the cybersecurity professional (and pre-professional) community for audiences who may know little to nothing about this rapidly growing industry.

So far, I’ve been able to interview leading specialists, entrepreneurs, and developers who have been enlisted by government agencies for their talents.
